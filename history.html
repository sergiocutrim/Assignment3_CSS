The combination of mint and chocolate has a long history, tracing back to the 16th century. Historians suggest that Europeans first combined these two flavors after their encounters with South American cultures. During that time, chocolate was commonly used in hot beverages, often enhanced with ingredients like cinnamon, mint, and various herbs and spices to balance its inherent bitterness.

The flavor combination reached new heights in 1973, when culinary student Marilyn Ricketts invented mint chocolate chip ice cream. This variety quickly became a staple in ice cream parlors and was even served at Princess Anne and Captain Mark Phillips’ wedding that same year, solidifying its status as a beloved flavor.
